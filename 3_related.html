Other Related Discussions

Ken L. Thompson, Reflections on trusting trust (1984) 🔗

  • This paper describes a compiler that can inject a self-reproducing Trojan horse into programs it compiles. The self-reproduction is achieved by having the compiler detect whether an input program is a compiler itself, and then produce a bugged executable of the input compiler that also contains this piece of malicious code. Any program that’s compiled by one of the downstream compilers will then receive a copy of the Trojan horse even if the program itself is free of errors.
  • The takeaway is that no amount of program verification done at the source-code level can ensure the end result will be correct.
  • A recent walk-through of this paper Adrian Colyer can be found here.

Terry A. Winograd and Fernando Flores, Understanding Computers and Cognition (1987) 🔗

  • The authors, Stanford professor Terry Winograd and Chilean philosopher Fernando Flores, take a similar stand to the one presented in De Millo’s, Lipton’s, and Perlis’ article. For Winograd and Flores, in real life program design is more a matter of learning of past failures and making changes based on them than on checking and creating consistency through a formal demonstration.
  • Programs are tools and their correctness depends on how well they answer to the needs of those who are designing and using them. Thus, “in designing tools we are designing ways of being.”

John S. Hall, Beyond AI: Creating the Conscience of the Machine (2007) 🔗

  • This book covers the history of AI and offers an assessment of the future of AI technologies.
  • In particular, the author coined the term “formalist float,” which refers to the difference between 1) symbolic representations of a problem and 2) deeper but partially non-symbolic representations that are required if one wants to solve the problem.
  • The author believes that formalist float is to blame for much of the failures in traditional AI research. This sentiment echoes that expressed by de Millo et al. - intuition trumps formal logic in terms of practical usage.

Apostolos K. Doxiadis and Christos H. Papadimitriou, Logicomix: An Epic Search for Truth (2007) 🔗

  • This is a comic about Bertrand Russell’s lifelong pursuit of the absolute logical foundations of mathematics and his struggles stemming from the conflict between an ideal rationality and the flawed fabric of reality.
  • Many of Russell's struggles were used as examples in discussions about the limitations and worth of program verification.
